About Our Club


In 1995, 37% of computer scientists were women. Today, it’s only 24%. The percent will continue to decline if we do nothing. We know that the biggest drop off of girls in computer science is between the ages of 13 and 17.


We provide a safe space for the girls interested in STEM in the Sammamish area. During the school year, we meet at Skyline High School before school on Wednesdays. Due to the current situation, we are meeting remotely on Mondays at 5:00pm via Zoom. See "Join" to sign-up and be on our mail list.

Our Values

MISSION: WE'RE ON A MISSION TO CLOSE THE GENDER GAP IN TECH. Girls Who Code is a national non-profit organization working to close the gender gap in technology. Our programs educate, equip, and inspire girls with the computing skills they'll need to pursue 21st century opportunities.

VISION: THE GENDER GAP IN COMPUTING IS GETTING WORSE. We're reaching girls around the world and are on track to close the gender gap in new entry-level tech jobs by 2027.

CORE VALUES


SISTERHOOD

We believe that diverse ability, culture, identity, and opinion makes our organization stronger.

BRAVERY

We believe being brave is about being resilient, persistent, and ambitious.

ACTIVISM

We’re not just preparing our girls to enter the workforce - we’re preparing them to lead it, to improve it, to completely and totally transform it.
